  • descriptionCapstone Cromosoma Project

    A direct application of my curriculum.

    Fall 2020 - Spring 2021

    During my senior academic year, I had the fantastic opportunity to complete a Capstone Project that fulfilled the final requirement for my Informaticsdegree. These projects can be wide and varied, but I had the opportunity to work with a team to directly make an impact with a small non-profit.

    My project was essentially to design, discuss, and implement technical systems for a Mexico-based non-profit named Cromosoma. This non-profit aims to helps small businesses in their area develop an online presence. Given the current times, this type of work is necessary to keeping local shops and businesses afloat.

    Hence, my team and I worked with Cromosoma to directly develop a system for collecting client information, storing that information, and automatically generating content (websites, marketing material, etc.) based off this information. The project has completed development and I can happily discuss it more, if requested.

  • personAssistant Instructor

    Teaching makes me smile.

    Spring 2018 - Current

    Choosing to become an Assistant Instructor at Indiana University was one of the best decisions I have made, which explains why it still is something I am involved in. At IU, I help instruct an introductory course for informatics (i101). This class teaches anyone who is interested about informatics as a topic and presents big-idea technology concepts paired with beginner coding skills ranging from HTML to Python.

    My responsibilities range from teaching lab sessions (usually coding lessons in various languages) to grading and monitoring student progress in the course. More recently, due to COVID moving our classes online, I have even had opportunities to create material being taught in the class and give insights into what I believe students should learn.

    From an experiential standpoint, teaching has truly shown me how awesome it is to see students advance so quickly in technical skills given only a single semester. Simultaneously, it is extremely rewarding to be able to see the progress of your teachings in the work around you. Ultimately, I would love to have a job that allows me to consistently see the great work that me and my team are doing. If you have more questions about teaching or would like a reference, contact me.

  • nature_peopleCareband Research Study

    Researching technology in medical care wearables.

    Fall 2019 - Spring 2020

    A recent project, that was unfortunately affected by COVID, was an opportunity I had to work with an Indiana University Alumni doing research for his company Careband. Adam Sobol (CEO) worked with IU to investigate his products (wearable devices for those with mental illnesses) as they were applied in rural homes rather than assisted-living centers, where they currently are deployed.

    My experience here was gained as I interacted with participants, delved into the process of research (using the IRB and writing/recording documents), and communicated across team members to find more individuals to recruit into the project and obtain insights.

    Unfortunately, COVID essentially shut this project now as we no longer could interact with participants, enter their homes to install hardware, or conduct other IU-related tasks. I was moved off-campus and since retired from the project to pursue other items.

    Contact me if you have questions about this project!

    A once-in-a-lifetime moment for college students.

    Spring 2019 - Fall 2019

    My time at Apple is one of the, if not the top, best experiences I have had in my working history. Due to connections with Indiana University, the company had a great program setup for college students who wanted to get involved in their team.

    My position title was College At Home Advisor and I essentially helped customers who called in with their technical issues. I had to problem solve, troubleshoot, and follow strict guidelines while also connecting and communicating with each person I met.

    Ultimately, the experience was less about the tasks and more related to the soft skills learned along with the experience with the company. Being able to be involved with such a large entity was both rewarding and eye-opening.
